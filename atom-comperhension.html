Understanding Atoms An atom has a nucleus at the centre Electron in shells around the nucleus The electrons and nucleus have opposing forces (negative electrons, positive protons) Atoms form bonds A single atom is an element-heading One gold atom = gold (element #79) One hydrogen atom = hydrogen (element #1) One unranium atom = unranium (element #92) What defines the element is the number of protons in the nucleus 1 proton = hydrogen 6 protons = carbon 79 protons = gold One atom is the smallest unit of an element Many atoms bonded together make up an element you can see Like a gold nugget, or water molecules Now let's go deeper inside the nucleus Protons (posivitve charge) Neutrons (no charge) Neutrons and protons are not fundamental! They are made of even tinier things called quarks Each proton = 3 quarks (2 "up" quarks + 1 "down" quark) Each neutron = 3 quarks (1 "up" quark + 2 "down" quarks) What holds these quarks together Gluons hold the quarks together The hierarchy is Quarks + gluons = protons and nuetrons Protons and neutrons = nucleus Nucleus + electrons = atom Atoms = elements Trillions of atoms = visible matter The fundamental particles (as far as we know) Quarks (6 types, or "flavours") Up, down (these make protons and neutrons) Charm, strange Top, bottom. These are truly fundamental - Not made of anything smaller Leptons (6 types) Electrons Muon, Tau (heavier versions of electrons) Three types of neutrinos (ghostly particles that barely interact) Force carriers (particles that transmit force) Gluons (holds quarks together - "strong force") Photons (light! "electromagnetic force") W and Z Bosons ("weak force" - causes radioactive decay) Graviton (hypothetical - gravitiy, not yet found) The Higgs Boson Gives other particles mass! Discovered 2012 This is the amazing part. All of these are not really "particles" - they are ripples in quantum fields!
